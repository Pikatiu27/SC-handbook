GUIDEWeld selection guideScenario-first defaults and limits+
Shear cleat / web plateDefault: fillet weld both sidesUse when load transfer is simple shear and both sides are accessible. Typical mark: 6 or 8 mm CFW both sides, SP.Avoid CPBW unless full-strength continuity is required. Check eccentric weld group if force is not direct.
Bracing gusset / cleatDefault: continuous fillet weldUse for gusset-to-member shear transfer where fit-up is practical. Typical mark: 6 or 8 mm CFW all round or both sides, SP.Prefer end returns where detailed. Check effective length, block shear path and local member wall/plate capacity.
Base plate to columnDefault: fillet weld around columnUse when compression bears through the plate and weld transfers shear, erection loads or uplift. Typical mark: CFW, SP, fuw 490 MPa.Do not rely on weld capacity alone. Check bearing, anchors, uplift, prying and column wall/flange limits.
Plate / member full-strength spliceSelect: complete penetration butt weldUse when the weld must develop member or plate continuity across the full thickness. Typical mark: CPBW, SP, fuw 490 MPa.Avoid fillet-only detail for full-strength butt continuity. Requires joint preparation, WPS, NDT and distortion control.
Partial-strength butt jointSelect: IPBW with specified awUse when a designed partial throat is intentional and full penetration is not required. Typical mark: IPBW, aw specified.Avoid for fatigue-sensitive or full-strength continuity unless specifically justified. Check preparation and inspection acceptance.
Moment flange / heavy welded jointSelect: CPBW or compound weldUse where flange force, restraint demand or geometry needs a butt weld plus superimposed fillet. Typical mark: CPBW + fillet where specified.Project-specific detail. Check through-thickness effects, lamellar tearing risk, access, sequencing and inspection.
Tube / SSHS cap or flange plateDefault: fillet weld where practicalUse 6 or 8 mm CFW where geometry, wall thickness and access suit. Upgrade to butt or compound where large fillets become uneconomical.Coordinate with fabricator for 10 mm+ fillets and closed-section access. Check local wall capacity.
Fatigue-sensitive detailSelect: fatigue-specific weld detailUse only after fatigue category, weld profile, finish and inspection level are specified.Do not select from capacity alone. Check AS/NZS 1554.5 and AS 4100 fatigue provisions.
Site welding / difficult accessSelect process after constructability checkUse simpler fillet details where access and weather protection suit. MMAW is common for site work; shop GMAW/FCAW or SAW is usually preferable.Confirm WPS, welder qualification, preheat, sheltering and inspection before relying on the detail.

Calculation steps

Calculation basis and limitationsAS 4100, AS/NZS 1554.1 and industry guidance+

Primary basis - AS 4100:2020 Clause 9.7 welded connections; throat-area capacity is calculated as φR = φ0.6fuwttlw with φ = 0.80.

Weld metal strength - AS 4100 Table 9.7.3.10(1), also summarised in ASI Simple Connections 2020 Table 2.14. Common strengths shown here are 430, 490 and 550 MPa.

Parent metal screen - indicative per-mm parent metal shear capacity is shown as φ0.6fupt using φ = 0.90 and AS/NZS 3678 / AS/NZS 3679.1 tensile strengths summarised in ASI Simple Connections Tables 2.15 and 2.16.

Welding process - process selection should be by the fabricator under a qualified WPS to AS/NZS 1554.1.

Symbols - welding symbols are shown in the AS 1101.3 style: the basic weld symbol below the reference line indicates arrow-side weld; above the line indicates other-side weld. Verify exact drawing symbols, preparations and inspection category to AS/NZS 1554.1 on the project drawings.

Scope - selected weld-type throat capacity under direct action: equal-leg fillet, CPBW, IPBW, or compound weld using user-entered effective length, runs and aw where relevant.

Design boundary - CPBW, IPBW and compound options are capacity views, not full joint checks. Parent-metal screen is not a tear-out, block shear, net-section, HAZ or eccentric weld group check. Parent-metal rupture, HAZ, joint preparation, backing/gouging, eccentric weld groups, intermittent weld rules, fatigue, seismic detailing, lamellar tearing and inspection acceptance criteria are excluded.
